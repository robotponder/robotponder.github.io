PONDER: Policy Optimization in Robotic Neural Dynamics Model for Real-World Adaptation

Anonymous Author*,
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Abstract

Deploying reinforcement learning policies in the physical world inevitably exposes agents to unmodeled dynamic variations, such as payload changes, friction shifts, or hardware wear, which deviate significantly from initial training conditions. Traditional sim-to-real methods, often relying on static domain randomization, struggle to encompass these unforeseen physical shifts, leading to performance degradation when the robot operates outside the randomized distribution. To address these issues, we propose a domain adaptation framework that performs policy optimization directly within a learned neural dynamics model. The dynamics model is trained on real-world trajectories to capture empirical system behaviors, utilizing diversity-driven exploration policies to ensure sufficient state coverage and thereby mitigate model hallucinations. We integrate the neural dynamics model into an on-policy reinforcement learning pipeline, finetuning a simulator-pretrained policy to align with the specific dynamics in the real-world environment. This allows the agent to recover performance under significant unknown physical variations. Experimental validations on bipedal locomotion tasks demonstrates superior adaptability and robustness to varying physical conditions, including significant payload variations and friction shifts.

Overview of the proposed domain adaptation framework. The pipeline operates in two phases: (Left) Simulator Online Pre-training, where a nominal policy is trained via a teacher-student architecture, while a neural dynamics model is learned concurrently using aggregated off-policy data; (Right) Real-world Offline Fine-tuning, where real-world interaction data updates the dynamics model specifically the proprioceptive encoder, creating a high-fidelity surrogate environment to fine-tune the policy against unmodeled physical variations.
